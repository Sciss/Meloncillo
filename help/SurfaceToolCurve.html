Curve Surface Tool

The curve or bézier tool (keyboard F3) is used to draw curvilinear transmitter trajectories.

First select the transmitter(s) to edit in the timeline frame's track list. Then make a timeline selection using either the timeline axis or the observer's timeline tab. Click+Drag on the surface to draw the initial line. A default shape will be drawn in light blue colour and can be adjusted by dragging on of the crosshair shaped anchor points. To render the shape hit Return. To cancel the rendering hit Escape. At any time you can activate or deactive the Snap function from the View menu. When activated, anchor points are snapped to neighbouring transmitter positions or receiver anchors (center points).

Two anchor points define the curve's start and stop and correspond to the timeline selection's start and stop. Two additional anchor points control the shape of the curve. They are similar to common bézier control points but 'exaggerate' the curve shape more easily in order to prevent the necessity to move control points outside the surface.

By default the movement will have constant speed. To enter variable speed mode, double click on the surface. The anchor points are replaced by new anchor points which control the velocity of the movement corresponding to the timeline selection start and stop respectively. A transparent yellow shape indicates the velocity. Dragging the anchor points farer away from the arc will increase velocity and vice versa. Velocity is interpolated between the start and stop point in order to retain the total timeline selection's duration. The algorithm will keep acceleration (dv/dt) constant.

To concatenate successive paths, hit Ctrl+Return. After the current gesture is rendered, the timeline selection will be advanced by its duration and the new initial drag is started automatically at the end of the previous path.

To prevent sudden jumps or 'clicks' at the timeline selection start and stop, activate the blending mode which will apply an adjustable fade-in and fade-out of the rendered paths.
