LISP Functional Extensions : calculating logarithm and exponential

(log <number> [<base>])
(pow <number> <exponent>)

these functions provide basic mathematical functions which are not included in jatha, taking the logarithm of a number and raising a number to a power of another number. when the base argument is omitted in a log call, the natural logarithm is calculated (base = 2.718...).

example:

    (println (concatenate 'STRING "an amplitude multiplication "
        "by 0.1234 equals a gain change of "
        (* 20 (log 0.1234 10))
        " decibels.\\nin an equal tempered scale, an interval of"
        " a minor second corresponds to frequency increment by"
        " the factor of "
        (pow 2 (/ 1 12))
        "."
    ))
